# Stanford Medicine

**Program:** PhD in Epidemiology and Clinical Research
**Department:** Epidemiology & Population Health (affiliated with Stanford Cancer Institute)

## Why it's strong for cancer prevention / population science
Stanford's Department of Epidemiology & Population Health lists **cancer epidemiology** among
its particular strengths (alongside genetic epidemiology, epidemiologic methods, and others).
The **Population Sciences Program** at Stanford Cancer Institute focuses on reducing cancer
burden and improving outcomes through observational and interventional research on etiology,
behavioral interventions, screening, and outcomes.

## Notable centers / strengths (verified)
- A **T32 Cancer Training Grant** program aimed at developing cross-disciplinary cancer
  genetic epidemiologists.
- Training grants supporting students with interests in cancer etiology, prevention, and
  treatment, plus data science and population health.
- A "Cancer Epidemiology and Prevention" course covering epidemiology's role in cancer
  etiology, prevention, and control.

## Why a student like Logan would want to train here
Stanford offers methodologically rigorous training with an explicit cancer-prevention and
population-science track and NCI T32 support, plus strength in genetic/data-science
approaches — appealing for a student who wants quantitative depth alongside prevention focus.
