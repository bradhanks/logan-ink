# MD Anderson UTHealth Houston (GSBS) + UTHealth School of Public Health

**Programs:**
- MD Anderson Cancer Center UTHealth Graduate School of Biomedical Sciences (GSBS) — PhD
- UTHealth Houston School of Public Health — Epidemiology / cancer prevention research

## Why it's strong for cancer prevention / population science
The **GSBS** is a 50+ year collaboration between **The University of Texas MD Anderson Cancer
Center** (the nation's flagship cancer hospital) and **UTHealth Houston**, letting PhD
students work with faculty at both institutions. Separately, **UTHealth Houston School of
Public Health's** Department of Epidemiology, Human Genetics and Environmental Sciences runs
cancer-prevention research, often in collaboration with MD Anderson.

## Notable centers / strengths (verified)
- GSBS admits "undifferentiated" PhD students who explore three first-year lab rotations
  before committing to a specialization — useful flexibility for an early-career student.
- UTHealth SPH cancer-prevention funding includes grants for **obesity-related cancer
  prevention** and an **NCI fellowship** training pathway.
- Direct access to MD Anderson's clinical and research scale.

⚠️ unverified: I did not confirm a single dedicated "cancer epidemiology PhD track" name at
UTHealth SPH via WebSearch; the cancer-prevention work sits within the broader epidemiology
department. Confirm the exact degree track on the program site before applying.

## Why a student like Logan would want to train here
For a student drawn to translational cancer prevention at the largest cancer center in the
U.S., the MD Anderson–UTHealth partnership offers unusual access to clinical scale plus a
public-health pathway focused on cancer prevention (e.g., obesity-related cancers).
