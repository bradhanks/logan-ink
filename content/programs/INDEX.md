# Target Graduate Programs — Cancer Prevention / Population Science / Cancer Epidemiology

A researched shortlist of strong U.S. graduate programs for someone (like Logan) aiming
to train in cancer prevention, population science, and cancer epidemiology. Each linked
file gives the program, department, verified strengths/centers, and an honest "why train
here" note.

**Method & caveats.** Every claim below is grounded in a WebSearch run during research
and cited in the per-program file. Rankings are notoriously noisy in this field; the one
quantitative ranking claim I could verify is that **Johns Hopkins Bloomberg was ranked #1
in Epidemiology by peers in the 2025 U.S. News & World Report rankings**
(per JHU's own page). I did **not** try to produce a numbered ranking of the others —
US News' full ordered list could not be confirmed via WebSearch, so treat the ordering
here as alphabetical/thematic, not a ranking. Faculty names appear only where WebSearch
confirmed them.

## Honest framing for an early-career applicant

These are competitive doctoral programs. For population-science / cancer-prevention work,
"fit" with specific funded faculty and an NCI-designated cancer center matters more than
overall prestige. The strongest signal is whether a program pairs a serious epidemiology/
biostatistics core with an active cancer-control-and-population-science research program
and training-grant (e.g., NCI T32) support. Each file notes those where confirmed.
