# University of Utah / Huntsman Cancer Institute

**Program:** Cancer Control and Population Sciences research environment at Huntsman Cancer
Institute (population-science cancer-prevention research; relevant graduate training across
the University of Utah).
**Affiliation:** Huntsman Cancer Institute (NCI-designated)

## Why it's strong for cancer prevention / population science
Huntsman Cancer Institute's **Cancer Control and Population Sciences Program** is a hub for
population-science cancer research, including cancer health equity and HPV-related cancer
prevention. This is the program tied to **Deanna Kepka, PhD, MPH** — a professor and CCPS
member whose work focuses on increasing HPV vaccination and reducing HPV-related cancer
burden, especially in rural and Latino communities.

## Notable centers / strengths (verified)
- The Kepka group conducts population-science research on cancer health equity and HPV-
  related cancer prevention in vulnerable populations.
- Kepka's training lineage runs through the **UW / Fred Hutch Biobehavioral Cancer
  Prevention and Control** fellowship (see the UW/Fred Hutch file), connecting Utah's
  HPV-prevention work to that broader prevention-science tradition.
- Recognized work: Kepka received an **HPV Vaccination Champion Award** for increasing HPV
  vaccination in Utah.

Degree pathway: the relevant doctoral program is the **Population Health Sciences (PHS) PhD**
at the University of Utah School of Medicine — its Cancer Population Science division and
Clinical and Translational Epidemiology emphasis connect students to Huntsman's CCPS faculty.
Confirm the current PHS PhD requirements and emphasis options on the program site before
applying.

## Why a student like Logan would want to train here
Given the Kepka tie, Utah/Huntsman is a natural fit for a student focused on HPV-vaccination,
cancer health equity, and rural/underserved population cancer prevention — a clear research
match rather than a generic prestige pick.
