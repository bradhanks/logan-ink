# University of Michigan School of Public Health

**Program:** PhD in Epidemiology
**Department:** Epidemiology (affiliated with the U-M Rogel Cancer Center)

## Why it's strong for cancer prevention / population science
Michigan's epidemiology faculty are integrated with the **Rogel Cancer Center's Cancer
Control and Population Sciences (CCPS) program**, which develops methods for cancer-control
and population-science research across the cancer continuum and studies environmental,
lifestyle, genetic, and socio-structural drivers of cancer risk and outcomes.

## Notable centers / strengths (verified)
- The **Cancer Control and Population Sciences** program at the NCI-designated Rogel
  Cancer Center.
- Confirmed epidemiology faculty in CCPS include **Lauren Wallner** (co-leads CCPS),
  **Andrew Brouwer**, and **Nancy Fleischer**.
- Trainees (undergraduate through postdoctoral) work with Rogel core members spanning
  basic, clinical, and population research.

## Why a student like Logan would want to train here
Michigan offers a strong epidemiology department coupled to a population-science cancer
program with explicit attention to socio-structural determinants and disparities — a good
fit for a student oriented toward cancer prevention and population science rather than
purely lab science.
