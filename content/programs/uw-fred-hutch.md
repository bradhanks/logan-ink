# University of Washington / Fred Hutchinson Cancer Center

**Program:** Epidemiology (UW) with research integration at Fred Hutch's Public Health
Sciences Division
**Departments:** UW Epidemiology / Fred Hutch Epidemiology Program

## Why it's strong for cancer prevention / population science
Fred Hutch's **Epidemiology Program** focuses squarely on reducing cancer incidence,
progression, and mortality by identifying risk/protective factors, elucidating biologic
mechanisms, and translating findings into prevention. Many Fred Hutch faculty serve as
training faculty for UW's Epidemiology and Biostatistics programs, giving students a
research home at one of the country's premier cancer centers.

## Notable centers / strengths (verified)
- Fred Hutch Epidemiology Program studies nutrition, obesity, alcohol, medication use,
  air pollution, and genetics in relation to cancer risk.
- A UW Health Services PhD **Biobehavioral Cancer Prevention and Control** training
  program (the same fellowship lineage that trained Deanna Kepka — see the Utah file).
- A UW/Fred Hutch jointly administered PhD in Molecular and Cellular Biology, with a dual
  PhD-MCB / MS-Epidemiology option for students wanting population-science breadth.

## Why a student like Logan would want to train here
UW + Fred Hutch is one of the strongest environments in the country for cancer prevention
and control, with a clear translational-prevention orientation and a notable biobehavioral
cancer prevention training pipeline — a strong fit for population-science cancer-prevention
work, including the HPV-vaccination/health-equity lineage relevant to the Kepka tie.
