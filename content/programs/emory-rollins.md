# Emory Rollins School of Public Health

**Program:** PhD in Epidemiology
**Department:** Epidemiology (affiliated with Winship Cancer Institute)

## Why it's strong for cancer prevention / population science
Rollins' PhD in Epidemiology trains students in epidemiologic methods across many areas
including **cancer**, social epidemiology, health disparities, and molecular/genetic
epidemiology. Cancer-prevention research is anchored by **Winship Cancer Institute's
Cancer Prevention and Control Research Program**, which draws on both the School of
Medicine and Rollins to detect cancer earlier and prevent it.

## Notable centers / strengths (verified)
- **Winship Cancer Institute** Cancer Prevention and Control Research Program.
- Confirmed Rollins epidemiology faculty who are Winship members include **Lauren
  McCullough**, **Joellen Schildkraut**, **Kevin Ward**, and **Robert Bednarczyk**
  (whose work includes vaccine/HPV-relevant epidemiology).
- Stated strengths in health disparities and social epidemiology alongside cancer.

## Why a student like Logan would want to train here
Emory pairs an epidemiology PhD with an NCI cancer center's prevention-and-control program
and notable strengths in health disparities and vaccine epidemiology — a good fit for a
student interested in cancer prevention with an equity lens (including HPV-vaccination work).
