# Yale School of Public Health

**Program:** PhD in Chronic Disease Epidemiology (cancer emphasis)
**Department:** Chronic Disease Epidemiology

## Why it's strong for cancer prevention / population science
Yale's Department of Chronic Disease Epidemiology is, per its own description, "perhaps best
known for its doctoral programs in the epidemiology of cancer," alongside aging, lifecourse,
genomics, and social determinants of health. Cancer epidemiology research at Yale addresses
causation, early detection, survival, and prevention across multiple cancer sites.

## Notable centers / strengths (verified)
- Research spanning hormonal and occupational/environmental exposures, infectious agents
  (e.g., *H. pylori*), cancer genetic epidemiology, energy balance/diet/physical activity,
  biomarkers, and public-health interventions to reduce cancer incidence and mortality.
- The GRE is no longer required (effective Fall 2025 application cycle).
- PhD students are guaranteed **five full years of funding** (tuition, 12-month stipend,
  basic health insurance).

## Why a student like Logan would want to train here
Yale offers a cancer-focused chronic-disease epidemiology department with strong funding
guarantees and breadth across etiology, prevention, and survival — a solid fit for a
student who wants a cancer-centered doctoral track within a well-resourced program.
