# UNC Gillings School of Global Public Health

**Program:** PhD in Epidemiology
**Department:** Epidemiology (affiliated with UNC Lineberger Comprehensive Cancer Center)

## Why it's strong for cancer prevention / population science
UNC's Department of Epidemiology is tightly integrated with the **UNC Lineberger
Comprehensive Cancer Center**, an NCI-designated comprehensive cancer center whose ~325
members span 40+ UNC departments. Lineberger has nine formal scientific programs, one of
which is **Cancer Epidemiology**.

## Notable centers / strengths (verified)
- The **Cancer Epidemiology Research Program** contributes to cancer etiology, diagnosis,
  prognosis, prevention, and survivorship research.
- Confirmed Gillings epidemiology faculty affiliated with Lineberger's cancer epidemiology
  program include **Andrew Olshan**, **Melissa Troester**, **Hazel Nichols** (co-leader of
  the Lineberger Cancer Epidemiology Research Program), and **Lawrence Engel**; other
  population-science faculty include **Jennifer Lund**, **Erin Kent**, and **Eboneé Butler**.
- Recent work includes geographic/disparities research (e.g., a cluster of NC counties
  with higher colorectal cancer death rates).

## Why a student like Logan would want to train here
UNC is a leading public-health powerhouse with a deep cancer-epidemiology and
cancer-survivorship faculty embedded in an NCI comprehensive center. Strong fit for a
student interested in cancer prevention, disparities, and population-based survivorship.
