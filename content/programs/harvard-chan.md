# Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health

**Program:** Program in Cancer Epidemiology and Prevention
**Department:** Epidemiology

## Why it's strong for cancer prevention / population science
Harvard Chan describes its Program in Cancer Epidemiology and Prevention as one of the
first comprehensive academic programs in the world dedicated specifically to training
cancer epidemiologists. The curriculum spans cancer research methodology, substantive
cancer epidemiology and prevention, and genetic epidemiology/biomarkers, integrating
biologic, environmental, and lifestyle factors into disease etiology and cancer control.

## Notable centers / strengths (verified)
- Integration into the **Dana-Farber/Harvard Cancer Center**, giving trainees access to
  unique large-scale data resources (per Harvard Chan program page).
- Faculty conducting research on lifestyle, environmental, and genetic determinants of
  cancer incidence and survival.
- Multiple internal and external funding/training opportunities noted by the program.

## Why a student like Logan would want to train here
For a student aiming at population-science cancer research, Harvard Chan offers one of the
deepest benches in cancer epidemiology plus access to landmark cohort infrastructure
through Dana-Farber/Harvard. It's an excellent fit if he wants rigorous etiologic and
prevention training at scale.
