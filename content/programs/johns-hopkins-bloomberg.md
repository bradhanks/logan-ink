# Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health

**Program:** PhD in Epidemiology — Cancer Epidemiology track
**Department:** Epidemiology

## Why it's strong for cancer prevention / population science
Per JHU's own page, Bloomberg's Department of Epidemiology was **ranked #1 in Epidemiology
by peers in the 2025 U.S. News & World Report rankings**. The Cancer Epidemiology track has
a long history of training masters, doctoral, and post-doctoral researchers in
population-based and clinical cancer research, with graduates moving into academia,
government, and the private sector.

## Notable centers / strengths (verified)
- Three integrated training sub-tracks: **cancer etiology and prevention, genetic
  epidemiology of cancer, and cancer control**.
- Signature courses including a two-term "Etiologic and Preventive Aspects of Cancer" and
  an advanced "Methodologic Issues in Cancer Epidemiology."
- An **NCI-sponsored T32 fellowship** supporting the cancer epidemiology track.
- Faculty research spanning breast, prostate, colorectal, and HPV-related cancers, plus
  health disparities and genetic/environmental risk factors.

## Why a student like Logan would want to train here
Hopkins pairs a top-rated methods core with a structured, well-funded cancer epidemiology
track (including NCI T32 support) and explicit cancer-control training — strong fit for a
student who wants both rigorous epidemiologic methods and a prevention/control focus.
