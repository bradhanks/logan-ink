# Huntsman Cancer Institute (HCI)

HCI is where the national funding ladder becomes local and accessible for a Mountain West trainee. It is an **NCI-designated Comprehensive Cancer Center** — the **only one in the Mountain West**, serving **Utah, Idaho, Wyoming, Nevada, and Montana** ([HCI / NCI](https://www.cancer.gov/research/infrastructure/cancer-centers/find/utahhuntsman); [HCI on Comprehensive designation](https://healthcare.utah.edu/huntsmancancerinstitute/news/2024/01/what-it-means-be-nci-designated-comprehensive-cancer-center)).

## Why "Comprehensive" matters

The Comprehensive designation reflects depth across basic, translational, clinical, **and prevention/cancer-control/population-based research** ([HCI research](https://uofuhealth.utah.edu/huntsman)). HCI runs **650+ research projects** and **300+ research teams**, including the largest Phase 1 trials program in the Mountain West. For population-science trainees, the prevention and cancer-control programs are the relevant home.

## Training infrastructure an undergraduate can use

- **PathMaker Programs for Cancer Research** — laboratory-based opportunities for high-school and undergraduate students (and teachers), often from **rural and frontier Mountain West areas** ([HCI research](https://uofuhealth.utah.edu/huntsman); [PathMaker Scholars](https://uofuhealth.utah.edu/huntsman/education-training/pathmaker/scholars)). The PathMaker Scholars summer program is 10 weeks residential, up to **$4,000**, funded by an NCI R25.
- **NIH-funded T32 institutional training** for PhD/postdoc trainees (see `cancer-center-t32.md`).
- **Faculty grants** that can carry **NIH diversity supplements** to support underrepresented undergraduates (see `nih-diversity-supplements.md`).

## Connection to Dr. Deanna Kepka & population science

HCI is the institutional home for Dr. Kepka's HPV-vaccination and cancer-prevention research and the **Mountain West HPV Vaccination Coalition** (see `mountain-west-regional.md`). For a population-science undergraduate mentored at HCI, the institute is both the research environment and the gateway to PathMaker, diversity supplements, and the broader NCI ladder.

## FAQ

**Is HCI the same as the University of Utah?** HCI is the cancer hospital and research institute at the University of Utah / University of Utah Health.

**What's the realistic entry point for an undergrad here?** **PathMaker Scholars** (Mountain West / rural focus) and a mentor-sponsored **diversity supplement**.

**Does HCI do population / prevention science?** Yes — its Comprehensive designation specifically includes prevention, cancer control, and population-based research.

**Who does HCI serve?** A five-state Mountain West region: Utah, Idaho, Wyoming, Nevada, Montana.
