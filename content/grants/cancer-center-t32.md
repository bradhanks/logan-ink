# Cancer Center T32 Institutional Training Grant

The T32 is how a cancer center builds a pipeline of funded PhD and postdoctoral trainees. It is **institution-held**: an NCI-designated center (like Huntsman Cancer Institute) or department receives the grant and **appoints individual trainees to slots** ([NCI T32](https://www.cancer.gov/grants-training/training/funding/t32); [Parent T32](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/contacts/parent_T32.html)).

## What this means for you

You **do not submit a T32 application to NIH.** Instead, once you are a PhD student (or postdoc), you apply *locally* to be appointed to an existing T32 program in your area. The program director selects trainees for the funded slots. So your path is: enroll in a PhD program → identify the relevant T32 → compete internally for a slot.

This is a `predoctoral` (and postdoctoral) opportunity at the trainee level, and a `faculty`-held grant at the PI level. **Undergraduates are not eligible.**

## Structure

- T32s may support **predoctoral only, postdoctoral only, or combined** training.
- A new or renewing T32 may request up to **6 trainee slots** per budget year ([NCI T32](https://www.cancer.gov/grants-training/training/funding/t32)).

## What it pays (FY2026)

- **Predoctoral stipend:** **$29,364/yr (~$2,447/mo)** — single level for all predoctoral trainees regardless of experience.
- **Postdoctoral stipend:** experience-based, set by years of relevant postdoctoral experience at appointment.
- **Training-related expenses (TRE):** ~**$4,750/yr** per predoc, ~**$12,400/yr** per postdoc.
- **Travel:** ~**$800** per predoc, ~**$1,000** per postdoc.

(Source: [NCI T32](https://www.cancer.gov/grants-training/training/funding/t32).)

## Caps and payback

- Aggregate NRSA support: up to **6 years (72 months) predoctoral**, **3 years (36 months) postdoctoral**, across any mix of T32 + individual fellowship ([Parent T32](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/contacts/parent_T32.html)).
- **Postdoctoral payback:** the first 12 months of postdoctoral NRSA support carries a service payback obligation (one month of qualifying service per month of support). *Predoctoral support does not incur payback.*

## FAQ

**Can an undergraduate be on a T32?** No — T32 slots are for predoctoral (enrolled PhD) and postdoctoral trainees.

**How do I get a slot?** Enroll in a PhD program affiliated with a T32, then apply internally to the program director.

**Does a T32 slot reduce my future F31 eligibility?** Yes, in aggregate — both count toward the predoctoral NRSA cap.

**Does HCI have T32 programs?** As an NCI-designated Comprehensive Cancer Center, HCI hosts NIH-funded training; verify current T32 offerings with the institute's training office.
