# HRSA — Federal Office of Rural Health Policy (FORHP)

HRSA is not a fellowship funder, but it matters to **rural cancer-prevention** in the Mountain West — exactly the populations the HPV Vaccination Coalition serves. Knowing it helps you understand where rural-health project money comes from.

## What FORHP is

The **Federal Office of Rural Health Policy (FORHP)**, within HRSA, administers state and local grants to strengthen rural health care and supports a rural-health research program ([FORHP](https://www.hrsa.gov/about/organization/bureaus/forhp); [Rural Health Research & Policy](https://www.hrsa.gov/rural-health/grants/rural-health-research-policy)).

## Cancer-relevant programs

- **Rural Health Care Services Outreach Program** — includes a Special Track funding projects that address drivers of rural disparities including **cancer** (alongside stroke, heart disease, chronic respiratory disease, injury, and maternal health) ([HRSA rural grants](https://www.hrsa.gov/rural-health/grants)).
- **Healthy Rural Hometown Initiative (HRHI)** — addresses the five leading causes of avoidable rural death, **including cancer** ([HRSA rural grants](https://www.hrsa.gov/rural-health/grants)).
- **Rural Health Research Centers** — publish rural-health research and policy analysis ([Rural Health Research & Policy](https://www.hrsa.gov/rural-health/grants/rural-health-research-policy)).

## Who holds these grants — and the career-stage reality

These are **organizational/institutional grants** — held by health systems, universities, state offices, and faculty PIs. They are **not individual student fellowships**. An undergraduate or predoctoral trainee engages only as a **student or staff member on a funded project**, not as the grantee. Tagged `faculty` because that is the realistic applicant stage.

## Relevance to population science

For HPV-vaccination and cancer-prevention work targeting rural and frontier Mountain West populations, HRSA funding can support the *delivery and outreach* side — complementing NCI's research grants and CDC's prevention programs. ⚠️ Note: specific *cancer-prevention research* dollars within FORHP were not enumerated in available search results; confirm current NOFOs at the source.

## FAQ

**Can a student apply for HRSA rural-health funding?** No — these are institutional/organizational grants. Students participate on funded projects.

**Is there a dedicated HRSA cancer-prevention grant?** Cancer appears as one of several disparity targets within broader rural-health programs (Outreach Program, HRHI); a standalone cancer-prevention research line was ⚠️ not confirmed in available sources — verify per NOFO.

**Why include HRSA at all?** Because rural cancer-prevention delivery in the Mountain West often runs on HRSA-funded infrastructure.
