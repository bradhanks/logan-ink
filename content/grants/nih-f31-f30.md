# NIH F31 & F30 Predoctoral Fellowships

These are the core NIH individual predoctoral fellowships (Ruth L. Kirschstein NRSA). They are a central rung for a population-science PhD student — the NCI runs its own F31 track for cancer-relevant research ([NCI F31](https://www.cancer.gov/grants-training/training/funding/f31)).

## F31 vs F30 — which applies to you

- **F31** — for students in a single research doctoral program (PhD) who are at or near the **dissertation research stage** ([Parent F31, PA-25-422](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/pa-files/PA-25-422.html)).
- **F30** — for students in a **dual-degree** program (MD/PhD, DO/PhD, DDS/PhD, DVM/PhD) who need support for both the research and clinical components. Dual-degree students are **not** eligible for the F31 because it does not support clinical training ([NIH F31 activity code](https://grants.nih.gov/funding/activity-codes/F31)).

## Eligibility

- **U.S. citizen or permanent resident.**
- Enrolled in a **research doctoral degree program** (F31) or a combined degree program (F30).
- For the F31, you must be a PhD candidate at the dissertation research stage by the time of award.

This is a `predoctoral`-only mechanism. **Undergraduates and post-baccs are not eligible.**

## What it pays (FY2025–2026)

- **Stipend:** a single predoctoral level of **$29,364/yr (~$2,447/mo)**, up from $28,296 the prior year — all predoctoral fellows receive the same level regardless of experience ([FASEB on NRSA stipends](https://www.faseb.org/journals-and-news/washington-update/nih-issues-updated-nrsa-stipend-levels-extends-esi-eligibility)).
- **Tuition & fees:** 60% of actual tuition up to **$16,000/yr** (predoc) or **$21,000/yr** (F30 dual-degree).
- **Institutional allowance:** to defray health insurance, supplies, books, and travel.

## Aggregate support cap

Individuals may receive up to **5 years** of predoctoral NRSA support (up to **6 years** for dual-degree/F30), counting any combination of F31, F30, and T32 support ([Parent F31](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/pa-files/PA-25-422.html)).

## Deadlines

Standard non-AIDS F30/F31/F32 deadlines: **April 8, August 8, December 8** annually ([NIH NRSA fellowship info](https://gao.fo.uiowa.edu/managing-grants-contracts/nih-nrsa-training-grants-fellowships/nih-nrsa-fellowship-information)). **Confirmed** as the standard cycle; AIDS-related applications use different dates.

## FAQ

**Can an undergraduate apply?** No. You must be enrolled in a research (or dual) doctoral program.

**Is the F31 only for bench science?** No — the NCI supports F31s in cancer prevention, control, behavioral, and population sciences.

**Does an F31 stipend stack with a TA salary?** NRSA rules limit concurrent compensation; check with your program. The stipend is set, not negotiable per-student.

**MD/PhD — F30 or F31?** F30 if you need support spanning both degrees; F31 only supports the PhD/research portion.
