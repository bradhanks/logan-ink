# American Cancer Society (ACS)

The ACS is the leading **private (nonprofit)** funder of cancer research in the United States — a complement to federal NCI funding, often with a distinct emphasis on **diverse populations and health equity**. Its extramural program is run by the **Extramural Discovery Science (EDS)** team ([ACS research grants](https://www.cancer.org/research/we-fund-cancer-research/apply-research-grant.html)).

## What ACS funds across the ladder

- **Undergrad:** **DICR Internship** — $500/week × 10 weeks ($5,000) of mentored summer research for underrepresented students ([ACS DICR](https://www.cancer.org/research/acs-center-for-diversity-in-cancer-research-training/diversity-in-cancer-research-dicr-internship.html)). See `acs-fellowships-dicr.md`.
- **Postdoctoral:** **Postdoctoral Fellowship** — $66k/$68k/$70k over 3 years + $4,000/yr allowance; deadlines **June 1 / December 1** ([ACS Postdoc](https://www.cancer.org/research/we-fund-cancer-research/apply-research-grant/grant-types/postdoctoral-fellowships.html)).
- **Faculty/investigator:** mechanisms such as **Discovery Boost Grants** (up to $135,000/yr × 2 yrs; max $297,000) — note ACS signaled a **pause/restructure** for the Summer 2026 cycle ([ACS Discovery Boost](https://www.cancer.org/research/we-fund-cancer-research/apply-research-grant/grant-types/discovery-boost-grants.html)).

## Diversity & population emphasis

ACS places **special emphasis on studies relevant to diverse populations** — age, gender, disability, ethnicity/race, geography, income, language, social class, and sexual orientation ([ACS Postdoc](https://www.cancer.org/research/we-fund-cancer-research/apply-research-grant/grant-types/postdoctoral-fellowships.html)). For HPV-vaccination, rural-health, and cancer-prevention work, this orientation is a strong fit.

## How an undergraduate engages ACS

Through the **DICR Internship**, applied for via a participating host institution (each sets its own criteria and deadline). The postdoc fellowship and investigator grants come much later on the ladder.

## FAQ

**Is ACS federal?** No — it is a nonprofit, funded by donations; it operates independently of NIH/NCI.

**What can an undergrad get from ACS?** The DICR Internship ($5,000 summer). Investigator grants are faculty-only.

**Does ACS fund population science?** Yes — and it explicitly emphasizes diverse-population relevance.

**Are investigator grant mechanisms stable?** ACS periodically restructures mechanisms (e.g., the Discovery Boost pause for Summer 2026) — verify current offerings at the source.
