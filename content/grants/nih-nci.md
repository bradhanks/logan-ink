# National Cancer Institute (NCI)

The NCI is the cornerstone federal funder for anyone building a cancer-research career, including in **cancer prevention and population science**. Understanding it as a whole helps you locate the right rung of the ladder.

## Scale

- **FY2026 appropriation: ~$7.35 billion** (Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2026), up $128M over FY25 ([NCI Budget](https://www.cancer.gov/about-nci/budget)).
- **Research Project Grants (RPGs)** — R01, R37, and similar — make up the largest share, about **43.3%** of obligated funds, across ~5,739 RPGs ([NCI Budget Fact Book](https://www.cancer.gov/about-nci/budget/fact-book/data/research-funding)).

## What it funds across the career ladder

The NCI supports fellowships in **all areas of cancer research, including cancer prevention, control, behavioral sciences, population sciences, and translational research** ([NCI training](https://www.cancer.gov/grants-training/training)). Concretely:

- **Undergrad / post-bacc:** Summer Internship Program, Student/Postbac IRTA/CRTA, diversity supplements (see `undergrad-postbac-onramps.md`, `nih-diversity-supplements.md`).
- **Predoctoral:** F31 fellowships, T32 institutional slots, R36-style dissertation awards.
- **Faculty/independent:** R01, R37, K-series, R25 research-education grants.

## Population-science home: DCCPS

The **Division of Cancer Control and Population Sciences (DCCPS)** runs funding for cancer-control, population-science, survivorship, and outcomes research, including **implementation science** ([DCCPS funding](https://cancercontrol.cancer.gov/is/funding)). This is the part of NCI most aligned with HPV-vaccination, cancer-prevention, and behavioral research.

## How an undergraduate engages NCI

You do **not** apply for an R01. Your realistic touchpoints are: a **summer internship**, a named role on a mentor's **diversity supplement**, and — once in a PhD program — an **F31** or **T32** slot. Match the mechanism to your stage.

## FAQ

**Is NCI only for lab/bench science?** No. DCCPS funds behavioral, population, and implementation science — directly relevant to cancer prevention and HPV-vaccination research.

**What's the biggest NCI mechanism?** Research Project Grants (R01/R37) — but these are independent-investigator awards, not for trainees.

**How does an undergrad get NCI money?** Through internships or a mentor's diversity supplement — not a personal grant.

**Where do prevention/population grants live?** The Division of Cancer Control and Population Sciences (DCCPS).
