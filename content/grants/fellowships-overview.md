# The Research Funding Ladder: Who Can Apply at Each Stage

Research funding is **career-stage gated**. The single most common mistake early trainees make is reading about a large award (an R01, an R37) and assuming it is a goal for *this year*. It is not. Each mechanism has a defined eligibility window. This overview maps the ladder so you can apply only to what you are actually eligible for, and plan the next rung.

## The ladder, bottom to top

| Stage | You are... | Realistic mechanisms |
|---|---|---|
| **Undergrad** | Enrolled in a bachelor's program | Paid summer internships: HCI PathMaker, ACS DICR Internship, NIH/NCI Summer Internship Program (SIP), Student IRTA/CRTA. Possible named role on a faculty mentor's NIH **diversity supplement**. |
| **Post-bacc** | Graduated, not yet in a PhD program | NIH **Postbac IRTA/CRTA** (intramural), diversity supplement (post-bacc candidate), research-assistant roles on funded grants. |
| **Predoctoral** | Enrolled in a research PhD (or MD/PhD) | **NSF GRFP** (first-year grad), **NIH F31** (PhD) / **F30** (MD/PhD), **R36** dissertation award (late-stage), predoctoral **diversity supplement**, a **T32** institutional slot, **R25** research-education programs. |
| **Postdoc / faculty** | Hold a doctorate | NIH **F32**, **K-series** career awards, **ACS postdoctoral fellowship**, then independent research awards (R01, etc.). *Out of scope for an undergraduate's near-term planning.* |

## Why the staging matters for cancer-prevention / population science

The NCI explicitly supports fellowships across cancer prevention, control, behavioral sciences, and population sciences — not just bench biology ([NCI Training](https://www.cancer.gov/grants-training/training)). That means a population-science trajectory has a complete ladder of its own: summer internship → post-bacc → F31 in epidemiology/behavioral science → dissertation award → postdoc. You do not have to be a wet-lab scientist to climb it.

## FAQ

**Can an undergraduate apply for an F31 or an R01?** No. The F31 requires enrollment in a research doctoral program at the dissertation stage; the R01 is an independent-investigator award. Undergraduates apply to internships and may be named on a mentor's diversity supplement.

**What is the first "real" fellowship a population-science student competes for?** Typically the **NSF GRFP** (apply as a senior or in the first year of grad school) and then the **NIH F31** once enrolled in a PhD program.

**Do these stack?** Some do not. NRSA support (F31, F32, T32) is capped in aggregate — up to 6 years predoctoral, 3 years postdoctoral ([NIH Parent T32 guidance](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/contacts/parent_T32.html)). NSF GRFP is separate from NRSA and generally cannot be held concurrently with another major federal fellowship.
