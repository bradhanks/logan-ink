# Undergraduate & Post-Baccalaureate On-Ramps

This is where the ladder starts. None of these require you to already have a track record — they are designed to build one. They are the realistic, `undergrad`/`post-bacc`-stage options.

## HCI PathMaker Scholars (Huntsman Cancer Institute)

PathMaker increases participation of young learners — including **rural and frontier students from the Mountain West** — in biomedical cancer research ([PathMaker Scholars](https://uofuhealth.utah.edu/huntsman/education-training/pathmaker/scholars)).

- **Format:** **10-week residential** summer research experience; live on campus, learn lab techniques, work on a PI-mentored project.
- **Compensation:** **up to $4,000** for the 10 weeks (amount depends on circumstances).
- **Funded by:** an NCI **R25** research-education grant ([grant record R25-CA240171](https://grantome.com/grant/NIH/R25-CA240171-01)).
- **Community:** integrated with NARI, GSRM, and SPUR summer programs at the University of Utah.

This is the most directly relevant on-ramp for a Utah State undergraduate in the Mountain West.

## ACS DICR Internship

Underrepresented-minority undergraduates: **$500/week × 10 weeks = $5,000** mentored summer research at a participating institution ([ACS DICR](https://www.cancer.org/research/acs-center-for-diversity-in-cancer-research-training/diversity-in-cancer-research-dicr-internship.html)). See `acs-fellowships-dicr.md` for full detail. Apply through the host institution.

## NIH/NCI Summer Internship Program (SIP) and Student IRTA/CRTA

- The **NIH Summer Internship Program (SIP)** places students with NIH scientists for **≥8 weeks**, usually starting in May, on the NIH campus; open to **U.S. citizens/permanent residents, ≥16 years old, enrolled at least part-time**; a stipend is provided ([NIH OITE stipends](https://www.training.nih.gov/stipends/)).
- **Student IRTA/CRTA** appointments give high-school, undergraduate, professional-school, and graduate students a developmental research experience, often in summer ([NCI CRTA](https://www.cancer.gov/grants-training/training/at-nci/crta); [NIH IRTA mechanism](https://oir.nih.gov/sourcebook/personnel/ipds-appointment-mechanisms/intramural-research-training-award-irta-hiring-mechanism-postdoctoral-pre)).
- Stipends are set by **education level completed** ([NIH OITE stipends](https://www.training.nih.gov/stipends/)).

## NIH Postbac IRTA/CRTA — the post-bacc bridge

For graduates not yet in a PhD program, the **Postbac IRTA/CRTA** funds 1–2 years of full-time intramural research at NIH. Eligibility (any one): bachelor's received **< 3 years** before starting; or > 3 years past bachelor's but a master's received **< 6 months** before starting; or accepted to grad/medical school with written permission to delay entry up to one year ([Postbac IRTA/CRTA](https://umgcccfundingopps.org/node/188)). This is the classic `post-bacc` move to strengthen a PhD application.

## FAQ

**Which should a Utah State undergrad target first?** **HCI PathMaker** (Mountain West / rural focus, local to your mentor's institution) and **ACS DICR** are the most direct fits.

**Do these pay enough to live on?** They are training stipends, not salaries — designed for a focused summer or a bridge year, not long-term income.

**What's the post-bacc move if I'm not ready for a PhD?** The **NIH Postbac IRTA/CRTA** — 1–2 years of full-time research to build your application.

**Deadlines?** Summer programs generally close in **winter/early spring**; exact dates vary by program and year — **verify at each program's site.**
