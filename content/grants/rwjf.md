# Robert Wood Johnson Foundation (RWJF)

RWJF is the major **philanthropic** funder of health-equity and population-health work — a different lane from NCI's research grants, focused on policy, systems, and leadership. For a population-science trainee thinking about **health policy and equity**, it is worth knowing, but it engages at the **doctoral level and above**.

## Health Policy Research Scholars (HPRS) — the trainee program

HPRS is a **4-year program for doctoral students** from historically marginalized backgrounds or underrepresented in their discipline, who want to improve health, well-being, and equity ([HPRS / RWJF](https://www.rwjf.org/en/our-vision/focus-areas/leadership-for-better-health/health-policy-research-scholars.html); [HPRS site](https://healthpolicyresearch-scholars.org/about-the-program/)).

- **Support:** annual stipend of **~$25,000 per scholar** ([RWJF active funding](https://www.rwjf.org/en/grants/active-funding-opportunities/2023/health-policy-research-scholars.html)).
- **Eligible disciplines:** broad — urban planning, political science, economics, anthropology, education, social work, geography, sociology, and related fields committed to policy change for population health and equity.
- **Career stage:** `predoctoral` (enrolled doctoral students) — **not undergraduates.**

⚠️ **Status note:** RWJF welcomed its **ninth and final HPRS cohort in September 2025** ([PMC on HPRS](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10339164/); RWJF). Treat HPRS as possibly **winding down** — verify whether new cohorts are being accepted before relying on it.

## Other RWJF tracks

RWJF also runs the **Health Policy Fellows** program (mid-career professionals/faculty) ([RWJF Health Policy Fellows](https://www.rwjf.org/en/grants/active-funding-opportunities/2025/robert-wood-johnson-foundation-health-policy-fellows.html)) and historically the Health & Society Scholars program. These are `faculty`/professional-stage.

## Relevance to population science

RWJF's health-equity orientation aligns with cancer-prevention disparities work (rural access, HPV-vaccination equity). It is a **future rung** — relevant once in a doctoral program with a policy/equity focus, not an undergraduate option.

## FAQ

**Can an undergraduate apply to RWJF?** Not to HPRS or Health Policy Fellows — these are doctoral and professional programs.

**Is HPRS still accepting applicants?** The ninth cohort (Sept 2025) was described as the **final** one — ⚠️ verify current status at rwjf.org before planning around it.

**Is RWJF cancer-specific?** No — it funds broad health equity and population health, which overlaps with cancer-prevention disparities work.
