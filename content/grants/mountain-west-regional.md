# Mountain West HPV Vaccination Coalition & Regional Landscape

This entry is about **context, not a grant you apply to**. Understanding the coalition explains *where* the population-science funding (NCI, CDC, HRSA) gets used in the Mountain West, and the network a Kepka-mentored trainee joins.

## The coalition

- **Led by Dr. Deanna Kepka, PhD, MPH** — HCI investigator and associate professor in the University of Utah College of Nursing; also Director of Global and International Health and inaugural leader of HCI's Global Oncology Initiative ([Kepka faculty profile](https://faculty.utah.edu/u0846780-Deanna_Lee_Kepka,_PhD,_MPH/hm/index.hml); [HCI press release](https://healthcare.utah.edu/huntsmancancerinstitute/press-releases/2025/03/deanna-kepka-phd-mph-appointed-inaugural-leader-of-global-oncology)).
- **~500 members**, launched **2014** (10th anniversary celebrated), spanning primary-care specialists, health departments, clinics, community and faith organizations, and parents across the region ([Mountain West HPV Vaccination Coalition](https://healthcare.utah.edu/huntsmancancerinstitute/about-us/hpv-coalition)).
- **Purpose:** improve HPV vaccination by strengthening collaboration among cancer centers, health departments, clinics, religious groups, and community organizations ([Springer / J Community Health](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10900-017-0334-7)).

## What it is — and is not

The coalition is a **convening, training, and capacity-building network**. It does **not** issue research grants. Its value to a trainee is the **research community, data, partnerships, and mentorship** it provides — the human infrastructure that supports grant-funded population-science projects.

## The regional funding landscape it sits within

HPV-vaccination and cancer-prevention work in the Mountain West draws on:

- **NCI / DCCPS** — implementation-science and population-science grants (see `nih-nci.md`).
- **CDC** — National Comprehensive Cancer Control Program and cancer-prevention funding (see `cdc.md`).
- **HRSA / FORHP** — rural-health funding relevant to the region's rural and frontier populations (see `hrsa-rural-health.md`).

Published intervention work tied to this community includes rural HPV-vaccination interventions during COVID-19 ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8521914/)).

## How a trainee engages

You don't "apply" to the coalition for money — you participate in its work and use it as the setting for grant-funded projects (a diversity supplement, an F31, a CDC-funded program evaluation). It is the **community of practice** around which the population-science ladder is built in the Mountain West.

## FAQ

**Is this a grant program?** No — it is a regional coalition/network, not a funder.

**Who leads it?** Dr. Deanna Kepka at Huntsman Cancer Institute.

**How big is it?** About 500 members across the Mountain West; founded 2014.

**Why does it matter for funding?** It anchors the regional population-science community where NCI, CDC, and HRSA dollars are applied to HPV-vaccination and cancer-prevention work.
