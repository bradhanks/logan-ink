# NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program (GRFP)

The GRFP is the oldest U.S. graduate STEM fellowship and one of the first major awards a population-science or public-health-adjacent student realistically competes for. It funds the *person*, not a specific project, which makes it portable across institutions and topics.

## Who can realistically apply

- **College seniors** intending to enroll in a full-time, research-based graduate program — this is the ideal first attempt.
- **First-year graduate students** in an eligible STEM field. As of recent cycles, graduate-student applicants are eligible **only in the first year** of their degree program ([NSF GRFP solicitation](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/grfp-nsf-graduate-research-fellowship-program/nsf25-547/solicitation)).
- You must be a **U.S. citizen, national, or permanent resident**.
- You must not have earned a prior master's, professional, or doctoral degree, or completed more than one academic year of graduate study (limited exceptions apply).

This makes GRFP an `undergrad`-accessible application (you apply *before* matriculating) and a `predoctoral` one (first year only). It is **not** open to mid-program PhD students.

## What it pays

- **$37,000** annual stipend to the fellow.
- **$16,000** cost-of-education allowance paid to the institution to cover tuition and mandatory fees.
- **Three years** of support, usable across a **five-year** fellowship period (you can bank "reserve" years).

Source: [NSF GRFP program page](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/grfp-nsf-graduate-research-fellowship-program).

## Deadlines

Deadlines are **field-specific** and fall in early-to-mid November. In the 2025 cycle, all reference letters were due **November 7, 2025**, with **Life Sciences ~November 10, 2025** and several social/behavioral and education fields ~November 12, 2025 ([NSF GRFP](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/grfp-nsf-graduate-research-fellowship-program)). **Confirmed** for the 2025 cycle; verify your exact field deadline at the source each year.

## Fit for cancer-prevention / population science

GRFP covers Social, Behavioral and Economic Sciences and STEM Education in addition to Life Sciences — so epidemiology, behavioral, and population-health research can fit. Choose your field panel carefully; it determines your deadline and your reviewers.

## FAQ

**When should an undergrad apply?** In the fall of your senior year, if you plan to start a research-based STEM graduate program the following year.

**How many times can I apply?** Generally once as an undergraduate/pre-enrollment applicant and once as an eligible (first-year) graduate student.

**Can I hold it alongside an NIH F31?** No — GRFP cannot generally be held concurrently with another major federal fellowship; you would choose one.

**Is it only for lab scientists?** No. Social, behavioral, and population-science research is eligible under the appropriate field panel.
