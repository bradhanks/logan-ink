# R25 (Research Education) and R36 (Dissertation Award)

These two mechanisms are often confused. They sit at opposite ends of who applies: the **R25 is held by a faculty PI** and serves students as participants; the **R36 is held by a doctoral candidate** near the finish line.

## R25 — Cancer Research Education Grants Program

The NCI R25 (Cancer Research Education Grants Program, CREGP) funds **institutions and senior faculty** to run educational activities — courses for skills development, research experiences, and curriculum/methods development ([NCI R25](https://www.cancer.gov/grants-training/training/funding/r25e); [PAR-23-277](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/pa-files/PAR-23-277.html)).

- **Who holds it:** the PD/PI must be an **established investigator (Associate Professor or above)** ([PAR-23-277](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/pa-files/PAR-23-277.html)). A student never holds an R25.
- **How a student benefits:** by joining a funded R25 program as a participant. (HCI's PathMaker summer programs are funded through an NCI R25 — see `undergrad-postbac-onramps.md`.)
- R25 programs may augment but cannot replace NRSA (T32) programs.

So for an undergraduate, the R25 is a `faculty`-held mechanism you access *as a trainee*, not something you apply to as a grant holder.

## R36 — Dissertation Award

The R36 supports a **doctoral candidate's dissertation research**. The best-documented version is the **AHRQ Health Services Research Dissertation Program (R36)** ([AHRQ R36](https://www.ahrq.gov/funding/training-grants/r36.html)); several NIH Institutes (e.g., NIMH, NIA) run R36 dissertation awards tied to workforce diversity.

- **Eligibility (AHRQ R36):** U.S. citizen, national, or permanent resident; full-time doctoral student in good standing in an accredited research doctoral program (health services research, behavioral/social sciences, epidemiology, biostatistics, health policy, nursing, and related fields); **all non-dissertation requirements (including qualifying exams) completed** by submission ([PA-23-196](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/pa-files/PA-23-196.html)).
- **What it pays:** stipend **not exceeding the current NRSA predoctoral level**, plus up to **$15,000** other direct costs; F&A capped at 8% ([AHRQ R36](https://www.ahrq.gov/funding/training-grants/r36.html)).
- **Deadlines (AHRQ):** **February 1, May 1, August 1, November 1** annually — **confirmed** for the AHRQ program.

This is a **late `predoctoral`** mechanism — relevant years from now, after qualifying exams.

## FAQ

**Can an undergrad apply for an R25?** No — the R25 is held by senior faculty. Undergrads *participate* in funded R25 programs.

**Can an undergrad apply for an R36?** No — you must have completed qualifying exams and be at the dissertation stage.

**Is the R36 only AHRQ?** No; several NIH Institutes run R36 dissertation awards. Eligibility and topic focus vary by Institute — verify the specific NOFO.

**Population-science fit?** Strong — the AHRQ R36 explicitly lists health services research, epidemiology, and health policy as eligible fields.
