# ACS Fellowships & Diversity in Cancer Research (DICR)

The American Cancer Society funds the full research career, but two tracks bracket where a population-science trainee engages: an **undergraduate internship** near the start, and a **postdoctoral fellowship** years later. ACS extramural funding is run by its Extramural Discovery Science team ([ACS research grants](https://www.cancer.org/research/we-fund-cancer-research/apply-research-grant.html)).

## DICR Internship — for undergraduates

The **Diversity in Cancer Research (DICR) Internship** targets underrepresented-minority undergraduates to build the cancer-research workforce ([ACS DICR](https://www.cancer.org/research/acs-center-for-diversity-in-cancer-research-training/diversity-in-cancer-research-dicr-internship.html)).

- **Stipend:** **$500/week for 10 weeks = $5,000** for the summer.
- **Format:** hands-on mentored research — laboratory, computational/database, or clinical.
- **Scale:** grew from 8 institutions / 32 interns (2021) to **32 institutions supporting up to 224 interns (2024)**.
- **How to apply:** **each ACS-funded host institution sets its own criteria and selection**, choosing roughly 4–8 interns. You apply to a participating institution, not to ACS centrally. **Verify the host's deadline** (commonly late winter/early spring for summer placement).

This is the `undergrad`-accessible ACS track and a strong on-ramp parallel to HCI PathMaker.

## ACS Postdoctoral Fellowship — for postdocs (future rung)

- **Stipend:** progressive **$66,000 / $68,000 / $70,000** across **3 years**, plus **$4,000/yr** allowance; an extra $1,500 in the final year for meeting/symposium travel ([ACS Postdoctoral Fellowships](https://www.cancer.org/research/we-fund-cancer-research/apply-research-grant/grant-types/postdoctoral-fellowships.html)).
- **Eligibility:** in a postdoctoral position at a U.S. institution; U.S. citizen or appropriate visa/lawful status; terminal doctoral degree held **3 years or less** at application.
- **Diversity emphasis:** special emphasis on studies relevant to diverse populations (age, gender, disability, ethnicity/race, geography, income, language, sexual orientation) — a natural fit for population science.
- **Deadlines:** **June 1 and December 1** — **confirmed**.

This is a `faculty`-stage (postdoc) award, listed here so you can see the ladder — **not** something an undergraduate applies to.

## A note on ACS investigator grants

ACS also runs investigator mechanisms such as **Discovery Boost Grants** (up to $135,000/yr for 2 years; max $297,000) — but ACS has signaled a **pause/restructure** of Discovery Boost for the Summer 2026 cycle ([ACS Discovery Boost](https://www.cancer.org/research/we-fund-cancer-research/apply-research-grant/grant-types/discovery-boost-grants.html)). These are faculty awards; verify status at the source.

## FAQ

**As an undergrad, which ACS program is for me?** The **DICR Internship** ($5,000 summer).

**Do I apply to ACS directly for DICR?** No — apply through a participating host institution, which sets criteria and deadlines.

**Is the postdoc fellowship something I can get now?** No — it requires a doctoral degree held 3 years or less.
