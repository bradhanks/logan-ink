# Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)

Where NCI funds cancer *research*, the CDC funds cancer *prevention at the population level*. For HPV-vaccination and cervical-cancer-prevention work, the CDC is a central player — but it funds **agencies and programs**, not individual trainees.

## Division of Cancer Prevention and Control

The CDC's **Division of Cancer Prevention and Control** (within the National Center for Chronic Disease Prevention and Health Promotion) runs the nation's population cancer-prevention programs ([CDC DCPC](https://www.cdc.gov/nccdphp/divisions-offices/about-the-division-of-cancer-prevention-and-control.html)).

## Flagship programs

- **National Breast and Cervical Cancer Early Detection Program (NBCCEDP)** — funds all 50 states, DC, Puerto Rico, 5 US-Affiliated Pacific Islands, and 13 tribes/tribal organizations to provide breast and cervical cancer screening to low-income, underinsured women. Since 1991 it has delivered **16M+ screening tests**, finding 80,000+ breast and 5,000+ cervical cancers ([NBCCEDP](https://www.cdc.gov/breast-cervical-cancer-screening/about/index.html)). At least **60% of federal funds must go to direct clinical services.**
- **National Comprehensive Cancer Control Program (NCCCP)** — funds all 50 states, DC, 7 territories/freely-associated states, and 7 tribes/tribal organizations to build cancer coalitions; has produced **65 comprehensive cancer-control plans** ([CDC DCPC](https://www.cdc.gov/nccdphp/divisions-offices/about-the-division-of-cancer-prevention-and-control.html)).

(Advocacy context: ACS CAN has urged Congress to fund NBCCEDP at **$265M in FY2027** — [ACS CAN](https://www.fightcancer.org/policy-resources/cdcs-national-breast-and-cervical-cancer-early-detection-program-saves-lives).)

## Who holds these grants — career-stage reality

NBCCEDP and NCCCP are **cooperative agreements/grants to state, territorial, and tribal health agencies** ([CDC-RFA-DP22-2202](https://www.cdc.gov/cancer/initiatives/nofo-dp22-2202.html)). They are **not individual fellowships**. A trainee engages as **staff, an evaluator, or a research partner** on a CDC-funded program — not as the grantee. Tagged `faculty`/agency-level.

## Relevance to HPV / population science

Cervical-cancer prevention (the downstream cancer HPV vaccination prevents) sits squarely in CDC's screening and comprehensive-control programs. For a Mountain West HPV-vaccination trainee, CDC-funded state cancer coalitions are natural partners — connecting directly to the work of the Mountain West HPV Vaccination Coalition (see `mountain-west-regional.md`).

## FAQ

**Can a student get a CDC research fellowship through these programs?** Not through NBCCEDP/NCCCP — those fund agencies. (CDC has separate fellowship pathways, e.g., ORISE, not covered here.)

**What's the difference between CDC and NCI funding?** NCI funds research; CDC funds population-level prevention/screening delivery.

**How does this connect to HPV vaccination?** HPV vaccination prevents cervical cancer; CDC's screening and cancer-control programs are the delivery infrastructure for cervical-cancer prevention.
