---
title: Mission
---

I want to help close the distance between what we already know how to prevent and the people still living without that protection.

So much of the harm in public health isn't caused by what we haven't discovered yet — it's caused by what we've discovered and failed to deliver evenly. A cancer-preventing vaccine that never reaches a rural county. A clear message that was never written in the language someone actually thinks in. I find that gap unacceptable in a quiet, durable way, and I'd rather spend my work narrowing it than admiring problems from a distance.

My part is small and specific right now: learn the methods well, study the communities honestly, and treat the people closest to a problem as collaborators rather than data points. I believe rigor and warmth aren't a trade-off — the most useful research I've seen is both. Prevention done right is invisible; the cancer that never happens leaves no headline. I'm okay working on things no one will ever have to thank me for.
