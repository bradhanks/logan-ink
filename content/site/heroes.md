title: People Who Shape the Work
---

A short, honest list of people whose thinking I keep close — some I've met, most I'm still learning from at a distance.

## Met

- **Dr. Deanna Kepka** — DHK · University of Utah College of Nursing; Huntsman Cancer Institute, Cancer Control & Population Sciences. Tenured associate professor who leads the 500-member Mountain West HPV Vaccination Coalition and HCI's inaugural Global Oncology Initiative (2025). She showed me what it looks like to meet communities in their own idiom — including Spanish-language fotonovela and radionovela interventions — instead of waiting for them to come to the data.

## On my list

- **Dr. Otis Brawley** — OB · Bloomberg Distinguished Professor, Johns Hopkins. For decades of clear-eyed writing on cancer disparities and the gap between what medicine can do and who actually receives it.

- **Dr. Camara Phyllis Jones** — CPJ · physician-epidemiologist and past president of the American Public Health Association. For teaching a generation to name how social structures, not just individual choices, shape who gets sick.

- **Dr. Sandro Galea** — SG · dean and epidemiologist (Washington University in St. Louis [confirm current institution]). For insisting that population health is about the conditions that make people healthy, and for writing about it for the public, not only the journals.

- **Hans Rosling** — HR · the late physician and global-health communicator behind Gapminder. For proving that rigorous data and unforgettable storytelling are the same craft when done well — the both-hemispheres ideal I keep chasing.
