# Smoking and Carcinoma of the Lung

**Authors:** Richard Doll, Austin Bradford Hill
**Year:** 1950
**Where:** *British Medical Journal*, September 30, 1950

## What it is
A landmark hospital-based case-control study (~1,465 male lung-cancer cases matched to
~1,465 controls across 20 London hospitals) that found a strong dose-response association
between cigarette smoking and lung cancer.

## Why it matters / what Logan took from it
One of the founding studies of modern chronic-disease epidemiology and of cancer prevention
as a science. Early-career, the lesson is how a well-designed observational study plus a
clear dose-response gradient can shift global policy — and how case-control design works in
practice.
