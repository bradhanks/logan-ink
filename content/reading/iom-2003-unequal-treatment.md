# Unequal Treatment: Confronting Racial and Ethnic Disparities in Health Care

**Editors:** Brian D. Smedley, Adrienne Y. Stith, Alan R. Nelson (eds.); Institute of
Medicine (Committee on Understanding and Eliminating Racial and Ethnic Disparities in
Health Care)
**Year:** 2003
**Where:** Washington, DC: National Academies Press (Institute of Medicine)

## What it is
The landmark IOM consensus report documenting that racial and ethnic minorities receive
lower-quality health care even after accounting for access and socioeconomic differences,
and tracing contributors across health systems, providers, and the clinical encounter.

## Why it matters / what Logan took from it
A foundational document for U.S. health-equity work, including cancer-care and
cancer-screening disparities. Early-career, the value is recognizing that disparities aren't
explained by access alone — system- and provider-level factors matter — which shapes how one
designs equitable prevention and screening interventions.
