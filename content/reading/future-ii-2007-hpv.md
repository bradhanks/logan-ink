# Quadrivalent Vaccine against Human Papillomavirus to Prevent High-Grade Cervical Lesions

**Author:** The FUTURE II Study Group
**Year:** 2007
**Where:** *New England Journal of Medicine*, 356(19), 1915–1927. DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa061741

## What it is
A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial in 12,167 women aged 15–26 testing the
quadrivalent (HPV 6/11/16/18) vaccine against high-grade cervical lesions (CIN 2/3,
adenocarcinoma in situ, or cervical cancer related to HPV-16/18). In the per-protocol
susceptible population, vaccine efficacy was ~100% for the primary endpoints.

## Why it matters / what Logan took from it
The pivotal efficacy trial behind HPV vaccination — the prevention intervention at the
heart of the Kepka-lineage work. Early-career, the key lessons are how randomized
prevention trials are designed (per-protocol vs. intention-to-treat, "susceptible"
populations) and why an intermediate endpoint (high-grade lesions) was used as a stand-in
for cancer itself.
