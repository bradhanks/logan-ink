# Global Cancer Statistics 2020: GLOBOCAN Estimates of Incidence and Mortality Worldwide for 36 Cancers in 185 Countries

**Authors:** Hyuna Sung, Jacques Ferlay, Rebecca L. Siegel, Mathieu Laversanne, Isabelle
Soerjomataram, Ahmedin Jemal, Freddie Bray
**Year:** 2021
**Where:** *CA: A Cancer Journal for Clinicians*, 71(3), 209–249. DOI: 10.3322/caac.21660

## What it is
The IARC/GLOBOCAN global snapshot of the cancer burden: an estimated 19.3 million new
cases and ~10.0 million deaths in 2020, with female breast cancer surpassing lung as the
most commonly diagnosed cancer and lung remaining the leading cause of cancer death.

## Why it matters / what Logan took from it
The standard reference for the scale and global distribution of cancer — the denominator
for any "why this matters" argument in cancer prevention. Early-career, it's a go-to source
for framing the population-level stakes and disparities across countries.
