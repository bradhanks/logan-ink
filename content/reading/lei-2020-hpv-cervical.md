# HPV Vaccination and the Risk of Invasive Cervical Cancer

**Authors:** Jiayao Lei et al. (Swedish registry study)
**Year:** 2020
**Where:** *New England Journal of Medicine*, 383, 1340–1348. DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa1917338

## What it is
A nationwide Swedish registry cohort of 1,672,983 girls/women aged 10–30 followed 2006–2017,
showing that quadrivalent HPV vaccination was associated with a substantially lower risk of
**invasive cervical cancer** — not just precancerous lesions. The reduction was larger for
those vaccinated before age 17 (~88%) than at 17–30 (~53%).

## Why it matters / what Logan took from it
This is the study that moved HPV-vaccine evidence from surrogate endpoints (lesions) to the
actual cancer outcome at population scale. Early-career, it's a model of how linked
population registries answer questions an RCT can't (decades-out cancer incidence), plus
the importance of age-at-vaccination — directly relevant to vaccination-timing and equity work.
