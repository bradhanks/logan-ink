# Modern Epidemiology

**Authors:** Kenneth J. Rothman, Sander Greenland, Timothy L. Lash (and, for the 4th ed.,
Tyler J. VanderWeele & Sebastien Haneuse)
**Years/editions:** 3rd edition, 2008 (Lippincott Williams & Wilkins); 4th edition, 2021
(Wolters Kluwer; authors listed as Lash, VanderWeele, Haneuse, Rothman)

## What it is
The standard graduate-level reference text on epidemiologic theory and methods — study
design, measures of effect, bias, confounding, interaction, and analysis.

## Why it matters / what Logan took from it
This is the book PhD epidemiology training is built around. Early-career, the realistic gain
is conceptual fluency: counterfactual thinking about effect measures, and recognizing the
three big threats (confounding, selection bias, information bias) in any study he reads.
It's a reference to grow into, not read cover-to-cover at once.
