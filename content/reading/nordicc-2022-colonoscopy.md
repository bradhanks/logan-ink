# Effect of Colonoscopy Screening on Risks of Colorectal Cancer and Related Death (the NordICC Trial)

**Authors:** Michael Bretthauer et al. / the NordICC Study Group
**Year:** 2022
**Where:** *New England Journal of Medicine*, 387, 1547–1556. DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2208375

## What it is
The first large randomized trial of invitation to a single screening colonoscopy: 84,585
adults aged 55–64 in Poland, Norway, and Sweden randomized 1:2 to invitation vs. usual care,
followed ~10 years. It found a reduced risk of colorectal cancer diagnosis but **no
statistically significant reduction in colorectal-cancer mortality** in the intention-to-
screen analysis (only ~42% of those invited actually got screened).

## Why it matters / what Logan took from it
A sobering, headline-grabbing screening trial. Early-career, it's a superb case study in
intention-to-treat vs. per-protocol, the role of participation/adherence, and why
"observational studies suggested 60–70% mortality reduction" can differ from a randomized
result. Exactly the kind of nuance cancer-screening policy turns on.
