# Social Conditions as Fundamental Causes of Disease

**Authors:** Bruce G. Link, Jo C. Phelan
**Year:** 1995
**Where:** *Journal of Health and Social Behavior*, Extra/Special Issue, pp. 80–94.

## What it is
The paper that introduced **fundamental cause theory**: socioeconomic status stays linked to
mortality across eras and diseases because SES confers flexible resources (money, knowledge,
power, prestige, beneficial social connections) that protect health *whatever* the proximate
mechanisms happen to be.

## Why it matters / what Logan took from it
A foundational health-equity framework explaining why disparities persist even as specific
risk factors and treatments change. Early-career, the takeaway is structural humility: an
intervention targeting one mechanism (e.g., a screening test) may not close gaps if the
underlying resource inequalities remain — central to designing equitable cancer prevention.
