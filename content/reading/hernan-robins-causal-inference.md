# Causal Inference: What If

**Authors:** Miguel A. Hernán, James M. Robins
**Year:** 2020 (Boca Raton: Chapman & Hall/CRC); the full PDF is updated regularly and
available free from the authors
**Where:** https://miguelhernan.org/whatifbook

## What it is
A modern, freely available textbook on causal inference from observational and experimental
data — potential outcomes, confounding, the g-methods (IP weighting, g-formula,
g-estimation), instrumental variables, and target-trial thinking.

## Why it matters / what Logan took from it
This is the bridge between "associations" and the question population scientists actually
care about: *what would happen if we intervened?* For an early-career reader, Parts I–II
are the realistic target — the potential-outcomes framing and why randomization and
exchangeability matter. The free PDF makes it the best self-study on-ramp.
